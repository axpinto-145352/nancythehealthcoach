TASK 1 Set Up Google Search Console

üìÖ Day 1 ‚è±Ô∏è 15 minutes üîß Difficulty: Easy
Why this matters: Google Search Console tells Google your website exists and lets you submit a sitemap so Google knows exactly which pages to index. Without it, Google may take weeks or months to discover your site on its own.
  1. Open Google Search Console.
    In your web browser, go to:
    https://search.google.com/search-console/welcome
  2. Sign in with Nancy's Google account.
    Use the business Gmail account (e.g., nancythehealthcoach@gmail.com). If you're already signed in to a personal account, click the profile icon in the top-right corner and switch accounts.
  3. Choose the "URL prefix" option.
    You'll see two boxes on the screen. Ignore the left box ("Domain"). Click on the right box labeled "URL prefix".
  4. Enter your website address.
    Type in the full URL of the website:
    https://nancythehealthcoach.com
    Then click Continue.
  5. Choose the "HTML tag" verification method.
    Google will show you several verification options. Look for "HTML tag" and click the arrow to expand it. You'll see a line of code that looks like this:
    <meta name="google-site-verification" content="abc123xyz..." />
  6. Copy ONLY the content value.
    You only need the part between the quotes after content=. For example, if Google shows you content="Abc12_xYz", copy just Abc12_xYz.
  7. Send that code to your developer.
    A placeholder tag has already been added to the website code. Your developer just needs to replace PASTE_YOUR_CODE_HERE with your actual code in the file index.html (line 5). Then rebuild and deploy the site.
  8. Go back to Google Search Console and click "Verify."
    After the site has been redeployed with the verification code, return to the Search Console page and click the Verify button. You should see a green checkmark and "Ownership verified."
  9. Submit your sitemap.
    In the left sidebar, click Sitemaps. In the "Add a new sitemap" text box, type:
    sitemap.xml
    Then click Submit.
  10. Confirm it worked.
    You should see your sitemap listed with a "Success" status. This means Google is now aware of all your pages and will begin crawling them.
What happens next: Check back in 3‚Äì5 days. Click Pages in the left sidebar to see which pages Google has indexed. It's normal for this to take a few days.
